✅ NutriSakti Master Confirmation Checklist
1. Core Mission & Demographic
[ ] Target: Specifically covers the First 1,000 Days (Conception through age 2).

[ ] Dual-User Focus: Features for both the Pregnant Mother (prenatal care, BPJS alerts) and the Mother with Baby (stunting monitoring, vaccinations).

2. The "Super-App" Functionality
[ ] Maternal Kit Marketplace: Decentralized request and fulfillment system for medical kits (thermometers, etc.).

[ ] Visual AI Nutrition: Photo-to-Recipe engine that identifies raw local materials and generates cooking guides.

[ ] On-Demand AI Video: Low-bandwidth cooking/prep videos for accessibility and low-literacy users.

[ ] Digital Health Book: Immutable record of medicines, growth charts, and vaccination history.

3. System Integrations & "Watchdog" Oracles
[ ] BPJS Kesehatan Oracle: Automated checking of insurance status with "Protection Alarms" triggered to local authorities if coverage is missing.

[ ] BGN/SPPG Verification: A "Nutrition Balance Check" that acts as an independent auditor of food provided by government partnerships, ensuring quality and bypassing potential incompetence.

[ ] 3rd Party Connectivity: Integration with WhatsApp/Chat for direct midwife/doctor consultations and video support.

4. Frontier Technology (The "Blockchain" Requirement)
[ ] ZKP Data Privacy: All highly sensitive medical/identity data is handled via Zero-Knowledge Proofs so it is verifiable but never exposed on the public ledger.

[ ] Open-Source Licensing: Entire codebase is committed to MIT/Apache 2.0 to qualify as a Digital Public Good (DPG).

[ ] Stablecoin Disbursement: Funds for kits and rewards are handled in USDC to ensure transparency and zero aid diversion.

5. Crisis & Emergency Resilience
[ ] Disaster Mitigation Scenario: A "Priority Protocol" that flags pregnant mothers and infants on the blockchain during natural disasters (common in Eastern Indonesia) so rescuers and aid agencies can prioritize them.

[ ] Offline-Sync Capability: The app functions without internet, using local storage and syncing via oracles once a signal is reached.
